Bitcoin Surges Past $72K as Geopolitical Risk Ebbs
--------------------------------------------------

Bitcoin jumped above $72,000 following a US-Iran ceasefire
announcement, triggering $280 million in bear liquidations. The rally
reflects a flight to risk-on assets as geopolitical tensions
temporarily ease, though observers warn the fragile truce remains
vulnerable to further escalation.

Why it matters: Bitcoin's positive correlation with ceasefire optimism
and inverse correlation with war risk demonstrates its role as a hedge
against geopolitical instability and currency debasement.

White House Admits Banning Stablecoin Yield Helps Banks, Hurts
Consumers
----------------------------------------------------------------------

A White House economic analysis concludes that restricting yield on
stablecoins provides negligible benefits to bank lending while
directly costing consumers in lost returns. The admission contradicts
financial establishment pressure to eliminate non-custodial savings
alternatives.

Why it matters: Government recognition that stablecoin restrictions
primarily protect banking monopolies—not financial stability—exposes
the true intent behind anti-crypto regulation: maintaining control
over monetary function and preventing alternatives to
fractional-reserve credit.

Treasury's GENIUS Act Would Give Government Power to Block Stablecoin
Transactions
----------------------------------------------------------------------

The proposed rule would require stablecoin issuers to allow the
Treasury to "block, freeze, and reject" transactions deemed illicit.
The framework effectively grants state permission to disable private
money without due process.

Why it matters: Centralized control over transaction
settlement—whether through banks or government-regulated
stablecoins—remains the core threat bitcoin was designed to overcome.
This rule codifies financial surveillance and arbitrary asset seizure.

-------------------------------------------------------------------
