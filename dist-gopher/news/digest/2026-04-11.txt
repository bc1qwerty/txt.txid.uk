Bitcoin's Quantum Computing Timeline Accelerates: Developers Propose
Solutions
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Advances in quantum computing are compressing the timeline for Bitcoin
network upgrades, but the Bitcoin Policy Institute and researchers say
the protocol could achieve quantum resistance without core rule
changes. A new proposal suggests Bitcoin transactions could be
inherently resistant to quantum attacks through cryptographic
innovation rather than hard forks.

Why it matters: Quantum computing poses an existential threat to
Bitcoin's security model, but the decentralized network's flexibility
allows developers to upgrade defenses without forcing
consensus-breaking changes.

Japan Upgrades Bitcoin to Financial Instrument Status
-----------------------------------------------------

Japan plans to reclassify cryptocurrencies as financial products under
the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act, shifting Bitcoin from a
payment-based framework to one aligned with traditional securities
regulation. The move signals stronger institutional integration and
regulatory clarity in a major economic power.

Why it matters: Major economies formalizing Bitcoin as a legitimate
financial instrument removes regulatory uncertainty and enables
institutional participation previously blocked by speculative asset
treatment.
